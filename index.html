🎓 Undergraduate Technical Guidance

Academic Year 2026

EGGSORT: A Multi-Microcontroller-Based System for Crack Detection, Weight Classification, and Packaging of Eggs with Telegram Notification
Core Focus: Mechatronics, Computer Vision, and Distributed Processing

This project tackles multi-stage agricultural automation. Because handling, weighing, and optical inspection happen simultaneously, using a multi-microcontroller architecture (like SPI or I2C communication between nodes) prevents processing bottlenecks. It combines load cells for weight grading, computer vision or acoustic sensors for crack detection, and an IoT gateway to push real-time yield updates to users via Telegram.

Drive ALERT (Driver Attention Level & Eye Response Tracker) Using B.R.A.I.N. Technology (Brainwave & Raspberry Pi AI Network)
Core Focus: Edge AI, Biomedical Signal Processing, and Computer Vision

A high-stakes safety system that blends physiological and behavioral tracking. It uses a portable EEG (brainwave) sensor to monitor cognitive fatigue alongside a Raspberry Pi-driven camera running AI facial landmarks to track eye closure (blink duration/frequency). The primary engineering challenge is real-time sensor fusion and minimizing false positives in a moving vehicle environment.

Arduino-Based CNC Styrofoam Cutting System
Core Focus: Precision Motion Control and Mechatronics

A classic Cartesian coordinate automation project. It utilizes an Arduino running GRBL firmware to translate G-code into precise stepper motor movements, controlling a heated nichrome wire. The core focus here is mechanical rigidity, precise thermal control of the wire to prevent melting/snagging, and smooth multi-axis interpolation.

Design and Development of a Solar-Powered IoT-Based Arduino Egg Incubator System
Core Focus: Thermal Dynamics, Renewable Energy, and IoT Feedback Loops

An off-grid agricultural system requiring precise environmental control. The system must strictly regulate temperature and humidity using closed-loop PID control via Arduino. The engineering challenge lies in energy budgeting—ensuring the solar photovoltaic and battery storage system can sustain the heating elements and automatic turning mechanisms 24/7.

Academic Year 2025

Eco-PET Filament: A Microcontroller-Based Pultrusion System For Recycling Pet Bottles Into 3d Printing Filament
Thermal Mechanics & Material Processing

A brilliant circular-economy project. It involves stripping plastic bottles into uniform ribbons and pulling them through a heated nozzle (pultrusion) to reform them into 1.75mm 3D printing filament. The microcontroller must precisely maintain the melting temperature and pull-rate speed to prevent variations in filament diameter.

Design And Development Of Anchovy Fish Dehydrator System
Food Engineering & Process Automation

A targeted agricultural appliance designed to replace traditional open-air sun drying. The system controls temperature and airflow to rapidly remove moisture from anchovies while preserving nutritional value. Key metrics involve moisture extraction rate (MER) and energy efficiency.

Safety Helmet-Ignition With Anti-Theft System For Motorcycle
Wireless Interlocking & Vehicle Safety

A safety system that forces rider compliance. The motorcycle's ignition system is hardwired to a wireless receiver that remains locked until an RF or Bluetooth signal confirms the helmet is securely buckled on the rider's head. It also integrates an anti-theft relay to cut fuel or power if unauthorized tampering is detected.

Academic Year 2024

Arduino Snacks Vending Machine with Thin Film Transistor Capacitive Touch Screen and Refill Mechanism
Human-Machine Interface & Electromechanical Dispensing

A consumer-facing automation project. It pairs a capacitive TFT touchscreen for a responsive user interface with an array of spring-coils driven by continuous rotation servos or stepper motors. The refill mechanism introduces an inventory tracking feature, alerting operators when stock is low.

Academic Year 2023

Smart Hydroponic Farming Technology: Arduino-Based Controlled Environment For Lettuce Cultivation with SMS Notification
Hydroponics, Water Chemistry, & Telemetry

An automated indoor farming system that keeps nutrient solutions at optimal levels. The Arduino monitors and self-corrects pH levels, Electrical Conductivity (EC), and water temperature. If any parameter falls outside the thresholds required for lettuce, it updates the farmer via GSM-based SMS alerts.

Nurturing Foundational Scientific Inquiry and Applied Prototyping (2026)

BINDUINO: A Sensor-Integrated Arduino-Based Smart Bin for Automated Solid Waste Segregation

Prototyping & Sensor Logic

An introductory automation project that uses inductive, capacitive, or optical proximity sensors to distinguish between waste types (e.g., metals vs. plastics vs. organic matter). The Arduino acts as the decision engine, triggering servo motors to route the waste into the correct internal compartment.

Growth, Morphological Traits, And Stress Responses Of Lactuca Sativa ‘Green Ice’ Under Varying Artificial Light Photoperiods

Controlled Environment Agriculture

A pure scientific study assessing how different durations of artificial light exposure (photoperiods) affect the growth metrics (leaf area, wet weight) and stress signs of Green Ice lettuce. It provides excellent baseline data for lighting schedules in indoor farming setups.

Repurposing Used Cooking Oil Into Sustainable Lamp Fuels: A Comparative Study Of Different Oil Types As Liquid Light Sources

Biofuels & Green Chemistry

A comparative material science project exploring the chemical viability of waste cooking oil as an alternative fuel. Students evaluate burn rate, soot emission, and luminous intensity across different oil types (e.g., palm, coconut, canola) to find the cleanest, most efficient fuel mix.

Enhancing Solar Panel Photovoltaic Efficiency Using Mirrors And Lens-Based Light Concentrators

Applied Physics & Solar Optics

An experimental research project aiming to increase power output without buying larger solar panels. By using geometric optics (mirrors to reflect light or Fresnel lenses to concentrate it), students measure the trade-off between increased voltage output and performance-degrading heat.

The Innovative Use Of Rice-Husk Waste For Manufacturing Eco-Friendly Food Packaging

Bioplastics & Circular Economy

A materials engineering study that takes an agricultural byproduct (rice husks) and binds it with natural polymers (like starch or gelatin) to create a biodegradable alternative to styrofoam containers. Testing centers on tensile strength, water resistance, and degradation rates.
